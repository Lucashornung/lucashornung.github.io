Lucas's Research Page!

Hello - I'm Lucas! I'm currently an atmospheric science PhD student at the University of California Davis. I'm originally from Houston, TX and graduated from Clear Falls High School (go Knights!) in 2022. After highschool, I attended Long Island University - Post (go Sharks!) where I played NCAA D1 tennis and pursued a dual-degree in mathematics and computer science. I had originally planned to pursue a career in electrical engineering (following in my father's footsteps), but after spending a summer volunteering for the Student Conservation Assocation in the backcountry wilderness of Washington state, I knew I wanted to be an Earth scientist. From there, I interned for two summers in the Cloud Processes Group at Brookhaven National Laboratory, fell in love with cloud physics, and the rest is history!

Now, my research is focused on using high-resolution numerical models to develop advanced cloud microphysics parameterizations and improve our theoretical understanding of cloud microphysics processes.
Outside of academics and research, I a most likely playing tennis (albeit far less competetively), spending time in the outdoors, strumming my guitar, seeing films in the theater, or embracing my German heritage (meist mit Bier).
It is my goal to enter academia and become a professor, so I am always looking for new opportunities to improve my teaching and presentation skills and mentor students. I'm happy to collaborate with current undergraduate students and help you realize that you also want to be a cloud physicist!

Thanks for visiting!
